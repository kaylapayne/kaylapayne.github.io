High Altitude Muon Detector

I led a team designing and building a muon detector to fly on a weather balloon at 30,000m. A design was optimized based on a detector developed by a group at MIT to minimize mass, volume, and power consumption while functioning at pressures of 100kPa to 1kPa and temperatures of 25ºC to -55ºC. I developed software to store muon detection-rate data collected as a function of altitude. I also oversaw circuitry design, Arduino coding, PCB assembly, scintillator crystal assembly, testing, troubleshooting and deployment, resulting in a successful launch in April 2024.

Lunar Rover Auxiliary Traction Mechanism

Mechanical and systems design of an auxiliary traction mechanism for a lunar rover, focusing on reliability in regolith, dimensional constraints, and integration with existing rover subsystems.
